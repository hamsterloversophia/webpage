Syrian Hamsters

Image credit: Petco

Syrian hamsters, also known as "Golden hamsters", are one of the most welcoming hamster breeds in the hamster world. They are very friendly and social to humans most of the time and will cuddle up in your hands if you are familliar to them. However, Syrians are solitary and naturally territorial. This means that even though they are quite freindly, you still have to be very careful with them! Refrain from putting two hamsters in one cage unless they are siblings as surely you don't want to see two injured hamsters when you wake up in the morning!

Their ears are pointed with dark coloration and the eyes are small, dark and bright. The average life span is two years, but these animals can live up to three years. The animal is 14–19 cm in length and weighs 114–140 g at adulthood. The female is usually heavier and longer than the male.

Dwarf Hamsters

Image credit: The Spruce Pets

This group of hamsters include the Winter White Hamster, the Campbell's Hamster and the Roborovski Hamster. Dwarf hamsters are a little more docile and fragile than Syrians. If you're planning to get one for a kid, make sure the kid knows how to handle these small, quick, fragile, and easily spooked animals. If not, you can always wait until they're a little older.
